Where did Cavaliers originate?

by Stephanie McGinty

  • Origin

Origin

Cavalier King Charles Spaniels originated in the United Kingdom and paintings of the little dog date back as far as the 16th century. They have spaniel ancestors and their predecessor was known as the toy spanniel. All modern-day Cavaliers actually originated from six dogs.

King Charles II was very fond of the breed, hence the name Cavalier King Charles Spaniel.

The standard for the breed wasn't developed until the 1920's. They were then officially recognized by the American Kennel Club in 1996.

More Info

Cavalier King Charles Spaniels were used as lap dogs for royalty. They also were used to drive fleas away from their owners.

Cavaliers have become quite a popular breed, especially in the United Kingdom, but also here in the United States. They have been featured in popular television shows such as "Sex and the City".
